IIP-TTS: Invisible and Implicit Punctuation makes Speech More Natural

Dam Heo
Neosapience, Inc. Younggun Lee
Neosapience, Inc.

Abstract

Punctuation marks have a considerable influence on the overall prosody and intonation of a sentence. One of the interesting features of the punctuation marks is that they do not have individual sounds. However, most existing text-to-speech models treat punctuation marks the same as the tokens like phonemes or graphemes. This leads acoustic features to be aligned to the punctuation marks, which is counter-intuitive. In this study, we remove punctuation marks from an input phoneme sequence to avoid mismatches. Meanwhile, we attach punctuation marks to the adjacent blank token or the end token to influence the overall prosody of utterances as an implicit bias rather than an explicit token. The results of experiments confirmed that the overall quality and naturalness of the synthesized utterances were improved with our model. This is also supported by the analysis of alignment between acoustic features and a phoneme sequence. Additionally, we showed that pitch control performance was improved more with our model than with a baseline model.
